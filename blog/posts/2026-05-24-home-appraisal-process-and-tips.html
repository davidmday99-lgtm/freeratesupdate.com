Home appraisal process and tips

May 24, 2026 • Michael Miller

An appraisal protects both you and the lender. Here's what happens and how to prepare.

  • What it is: A licensed appraiser inspects the property and compares it to similar homes in the area to estimate current market value.
  • Who orders it: Your lender orders (and pays for) the appraisal after you go under contract—usually $400–$600.
  • Timeline: Most appraisals happen within 5–10 days of the order and take 30–45 minutes on-site.
  • Why it matters: If the appraisal comes in low, the contract price may be renegotiated or you'll need to cover the difference with extra cash.

Tips to help ensure a strong appraisal:

  • Clean and declutter before the appraiser arrives—curb appeal counts.
  • Have repair records and recent improvements ready (roof, HVAC, foundation work).
  • Make sure the property is accessible and safe to inspect (trim bushes blocking windows, secure loose gates).
  • Provide accurate square footage and room count if questioned.
  • If comparable sales were lower recently, don't assume the worst—the appraiser sees the full market picture.

If the appraisal is lower than expected: Ask your lender if you can challenge it with new comparables, or explore renegotiating the purchase price. Some sellers will adjust if market conditions have shifted. This is a moment to be practical and know your options.
