First-time homebuyer essentials: don't skip these steps

June 23, 2026 · 6 min read

Buying your first home is exciting, but it's easy to miss critical steps that could cost you thousands. Here's the complete checklist first-time buyers must tackle before signing any loan documents.

Step 1: Check your credit report

Your credit report determines your interest rate more than almost any other factor. Before you start house hunting, pull your free credit report from AnnualCreditReport.com and review it carefully.

  • Check for errors or fraudulent accounts
  • Dispute any inaccuracies immediately
  • Note your current credit score range
  • Understand what's pulling your score down

Step 2: Improve your credit score (if needed)

Even small score improvements can save you tens of thousands in interest over the life of your loan. Six months before you plan to buy is ideal timing to:

  • Pay down credit card balances (aim for under 30% utilization)
  • Make all payments on time
  • Avoid opening new credit accounts
  • Don't close old credit cards (age of accounts matters)

Step 3: Get pre-approved (not just pre-qualified)

Pre-qualification is just an estimate. Pre-approval is a lender's guarantee that you qualify for a specific loan amount. This matters because:

  • Real estate agents take pre-approval seriously
  • Sellers won't negotiate with pre-qualified buyers
  • You'll know your actual budget beforehand
  • Pre-approval includes documented income verification

Step 4: Save for a down payment

You don't need 20% down to buy a home—FHA loans allow just 3.5%, and many conventional loans accept 3%-5%. But even small down payments require legitimate savings:

  • Don't take gifts from non-relatives
  • Document where gift funds come from (if applicable)
  • Show 2 months of bank statements proving the funds are yours
  • Large deposits near closing will be scrutinized

Step 5: Get organized with documentation

Lenders will ask for tax returns, pay stubs, bank statements, and more. Preparing these early speeds up the approval process:

  • Last 2 years of tax returns
  • Last 30 days of pay stubs
  • Last 2-3 months of bank/investment statements
  • Employment verification letters
  • Debt statements (student loans, car loans, credit cards)

Step 6: Avoid major financial changes

Once you're pre-approved, don't rock the boat. Lenders will typically re-verify your finances right before closing. Changes to watch out for:

  • Don't change jobs (or expect delays)
  • Don't rack up new debt
  • Don't miss payments
  • Don't make large deposits without explanation

Step 7: Shop with multiple lenders

Even a 0.25% difference in interest rates translates to significant savings over 30 years. Compare at least 3-4 lenders and don't settle for the first offer. Get written Loan Estimates from each.

Step 8: Understand your loan options

Different loan types (FHA, VA, conventional, USDA) have different rates, requirements, and benefits. Understanding which fits your situation helps you negotiate better terms.
