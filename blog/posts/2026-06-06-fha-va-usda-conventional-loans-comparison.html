FHA, VA, USDA, and conventional loans: how they compare

June 6, 2026 • Michael Miller

When you're shopping for a mortgage, your choice of loan type can be just as important as the interest rate. Each has different rules, costs, and benefits. Here's what separates them:

Conventional Loans

  • The "standard" mortgage. Offered by banks and lenders directly.
  • Down payment: typically 3–20%. Less than 20% means PMI (mortgage insurance) applies.
  • Credit score: usually 620+ minimum, though better rates above 740.
  • No geographic or employment restrictions.
  • Faster closing, fewer hoops. Straightforward underwriting.

FHA Loans (Federal Housing Administration)

  • Designed to help first-time and rebuilding buyers. Insured by the federal government.
  • Down payment: as low as 3.5%. Higher credit scores can go lower.
  • Credit score: FHA accepts 580+, some lenders will go to 500.
  • Mortgage insurance is required (both upfront and annual).
  • Debt-to-income limits are more flexible than conventional (43–50% acceptable).
  • Takes longer to close due to extra compliance steps.

VA Loans (U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs)

  • Exclusive benefit for active-duty military, veterans, and surviving spouses.
  • Down payment: zero required. 100% financing available.
  • Credit score: VA doesn't set a minimum, but lenders typically want 620+.
  • No mortgage insurance—VA guarantee replaces it.
  • Funding fee applies (1–3.6% depending on down payment and service history).
  • Often the most favorable rates and terms. Strong income flexibility.

USDA Loans (U.S. Department of Agriculture)

  • For rural and suburban buyers. Income limits apply (varies by location).
  • Down payment: zero required. 100% financing in eligible areas.
  • Credit score: 580+ typical, some flexibility for compensating factors.
  • Mortgage insurance applies (both upfront and annual).
  • Must be a primary residence in an eligible rural area.
  • Competitive rates, especially for qualified rural borrowers.

The Real Comparison

Think about your situation: If you're a first-time buyer with limited down payment savings, FHA gets you in the door at 3.5% down. If you're a veteran, VA loans typically beat FHA and conventional on cost and flexibility. If you're buying outside the city, USDA can mean 0% down and no PMI. Conventional works best when you have 20% down, solid credit, and want to avoid government-backed insurance.

The "best" loan isn't about which one sounds fanciest. It's about which one matches your life: your service, your income, your location, and your cash on hand. Each path has real advantages—pick the one that fits.
