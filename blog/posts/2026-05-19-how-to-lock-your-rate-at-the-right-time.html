How to lock your rate at the right time

May 19, 2026 • Michael Miller

Timing a mortgage rate lock is one of the most practical decisions you'll make. Get it right, and you sleep easier knowing your rate is protected. Get it wrong, and you could leave money on the table—or worse, miss a deal altogether.

Here's what you need to understand:

  • A rate lock freezes your interest rate for a set period—typically 30, 45, or 60 days. Your lender guarantees that rate, even if rates jump during that window.
  • Lock too early and you might watch rates drop and feel stuck. Most locks are non-cancellable (though some lenders offer "float-down" options for a fee).
  • Lock too late and rates could climb above your rate lock deadline, leaving you unprotected if closing delays happen.
  • Typical cost: Between 0.25% and 1% of your loan amount, though many lenders roll this into your rate.

When to lock (the real answer): Lock when you're comfortable with the rate AND you have a clear, achievable timeline to close. If you're pre-approved and within 30–45 days of closing, locking makes sense. If closing is 90 days away, you've got flexibility to wait.

Ask your lender these questions before locking:

  • What's included in this rate? (Points, fees, origination costs?)
  • How many days is the lock period?
  • What happens if I extend? (Many lenders charge 0.125% to 0.375% per 15-day extension.)
  • Do you offer a "float down"? (If rates drop 0.25% or more after I lock, can I refinance into the lower rate?)
  • What are the true closing costs with this rate locked in?

Real-world example: You're buying a home, appraisal is scheduled, inspection is clear, and you're 35 days from closing. Rates have been hovering around 6.5%. Lock it. You have enough runway to close, and you're eliminating the risk that rates spike. If rates drop 0.5% before closing and your lender offers a float-down, ask for it—you might save $100–$150 per month on payments.

The "perfect" lock doesn't exist. But the right lock is the one that matches your timeline and keeps you focused on closing without stress.
