Mortgage pre-approval process: what to expect

Wednesday, May 21, 2026 • Michael Miller

Pre-approval is your first real step toward homeownership. It tells sellers you're serious, gives you a clear budget, and locks in rates for 60–120 days. Here's what happens inside the process.

Why start with pre-approval?

  • You discover your actual lending power (not just estimates online).
  • Your offer holds more weight when sellers see a pre-approval letter.
  • You avoid the shock of being turned down after falling in love with a home.
  • Rate locks give you breathing room to shop without market jitters.

What lenders check during pre-approval

  • Credit score: Typical range: 620–680+ for conventional; 500+ for FHA. Higher scores unlock better rates.
  • Income and employment: W-2s, recent pay stubs, tax returns (usually 2 years). Self-employed? Plan for extra documentation.
  • Debt-to-income ratio: Most lenders cap this at 43–50%, meaning your monthly debt payments can't exceed that percentage of gross income. A $60,000 annual income with $2,000 monthly debt already uses up most of your room.
  • Assets and savings: Bank statements, retirement accounts, investment accounts. Lenders want proof you can close and cover reserves.
  • Down payment source: Documentation proving your down payment is yours (not borrowed). Gift letters required if family is helping.

Documents you'll need

  • Photo ID and Social Security number.
  • Last 2 months of pay stubs and bank statements (all accounts).
  • Last 2 years of tax returns (full with schedules if self-employed).
  • Explanation letters for any late payments, gaps in employment, or recent large deposits.
  • If self-employed: profit/loss statement, business license, corporate structure docs.

The timeline

  • Day 1: Application, credit pull, initial document request.
  • Day 2–3: Underwriter reviews application, orders appraisal and flood check (for future property once you find one).
  • Day 3–5: Conditions issued—requests for clarifications, additional docs, or corrections.
  • Day 5–7: Pre-approval letter issued. At this point, you're cleared to make offers.

Smart moves during pre-approval

  • Lock your rate now if you love what you see. Most locks last 60–120 days—plenty of time to search.
  • Don't make big purchases or open new credit lines. Your credit score gets pulled; any change could disqualify you.
  • Ask about your lender's approval timeline for the final loan after you've found a property. Expect 30–45 days total.
  • Clarify costs upfront: Does the lender charge for appraisals, credit checks, or processing? Can you lock-in costs on the Loan Estimate?

Pre-approval vs. pre-qualification

Pre-qualification is informal—based on what you tell the lender, no verification. Pre-approval is the real deal: verified income, assets, and credit. Always aim for pre-approval before making an offer.
