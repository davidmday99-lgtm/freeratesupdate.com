Mortgage rate lock timing strategy

June 2, 2026 • Michael Miller

Locking your mortgage rate at the right time can save you thousands over the life of your loan. Lock too early and rates might drop further. Lock too late and they might rise. Here's how to time it right.

Why timing matters:

  • A 0.25% difference on a $400,000 loan equals about $60/month or $21,600 over 30 years
  • Most locks last 30-60 days; going beyond that usually costs extra
  • Rate locks are free during the standard lock period—but timing wrong costs you the opportunity

Signs it's time to lock:

  • Stable or rising rate environment: If the Federal Reserve is signaling rate hikes or economic data is strong, lock sooner rather than later
  • You're within 30 days of closing: Most lenders offer free 30-day locks; use this window strategically
  • Rate has dropped to your target: If you've been watching rates and they hit your ideal number, lock it—don't gamble on going lower
  • Market volatility is high: During uncertain times, locking provides peace of mind even if rates might drop slightly more

Signs to float (wait to lock):

  • Clear downward rate trend: If the Fed is cutting rates or economic data is weak across multiple indicators
  • You're more than 60 days from closing: Paying for an extended lock period might not be worth it if rates are trending down
  • Your application isn't ready: If you haven't submitted all documents or have credit issues to resolve, wait until you're loan-ready

The 30-day sweet spot: Most lenders offer free rate locks for 30 days. Time your lock so that your 30-day window covers your expected closing date with a few days of buffer. For example, if you expect to close June 30, lock around June 1-5.

What if rates drop after you lock? Most lenders offer a "float down" option for a fee (typically 0.25-0.50% of the loan amount). Some offer one free float down if rates drop significantly (usually 0.50% or more). Ask about this when locking.

The psychological factor: Don't let FOMO (fear of missing out) on lower rates cause you to miss your target rate. If rates have been rising and suddenly drop to where you wanted them, lock immediately. Perfection is the enemy of good when it comes to rate locks.
