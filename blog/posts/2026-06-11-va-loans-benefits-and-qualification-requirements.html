VA loans: benefits and qualification requirements

June 11, 2026 • Michael Miller

VA loans are one of the most powerful benefits available to military service members and veterans. Yet many borrowers don't fully understand what they offer or how to use them. Here's the complete breakdown:

What Is a VA Loan?

A VA loan is a mortgage guaranteed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs. The guarantee means:

  • If you default, the VA reimburses the lender for the loss. This guarantee lets lenders offer better terms to veterans.
  • You're not getting a direct loan from the VA—a private lender funds it. The VA just backs it.
  • The VA backing is available throughout your entire loan life, not just at origination.

Key VA Loan Benefits

1. No Down Payment Required

  • You can finance 100% of the home's purchase price (or appraised value, whichever is lower).
  • This benefit applies to your first VA loan, but you can also reuse it after paying off a prior VA loan.
  • You can even get a second VA loan while the first is still active (though not on the same property).

2. No Mortgage Insurance (PMI)

  • With conventional loans, if you put down less than 20%, you pay PMI (typically 0.5–1% annually).
  • VA loans have no mortgage insurance, period. You save $200–$400/month on a $300,000 loan.
  • This is one of the biggest financial advantages of VA loans.

3. Lower Interest Rates

  • VA loans typically offer 0.25–0.5% lower rates than conventional loans.
  • Over 30 years, a 0.375% rate difference on a $300,000 loan means $100,000+ in savings.
  • Lower rates + no down payment + no PMI = substantial long-term savings.

4. No Prepayment Penalties

  • Pay off your VA loan early without penalty. Some conventional loans charge 1–3% of your balance to pay off early.
  • You can refinance your VA loan anytime without penalty.

5. VA Funding Fee (Usually Minimal)

  • There is a VA funding fee (typically 1.4–2.3% of the loan amount for first-time users, lower for repeat users).
  • This fee is upfront but is typically rolled into the loan balance, not paid at closing.
  • The funding fee is waived for borrowers with service-connected disabilities rated at 10% or higher.

6. Flexible Debt-to-Income Ratios

  • Conventional loans typically require a debt-to-income (DTI) ratio of 43% or less.
  • VA loans allow DTI up to 50–60%, depending on the lender and your compensating factors (savings, credit score, etc.).
  • This means you can qualify for more even with existing debt.

Who Qualifies for a VA Loan?

Active-Duty Service Members

  • All branches of the military (Army, Navy, Air Force, Marines, Space Force, Coast Guard).
  • Only 90+ days of active duty required (some exceptions for service-connected disabilities).

Veterans

  • Honorably discharged after at least 181 days of active duty (or 90+ days if discharged for a service-connected disability).
  • Age doesn't matter—even recent retirees qualify.

Surviving Spouses or Former Spouses

  • Surviving spouse of service member/veteran who died from a service-connected disability or in active duty.
  • Former spouses may qualify under specific circumstances (VA rules apply).

National Guard and Reserves

  • 6+ years of service (usually), or less if called to active duty and served 90+ days.
  • Requirements vary by branch.

How to Get Your Certificate of Eligibility (COE)

You need a COE to apply for a VA loan:

  • Online: Visit VA.gov, log in, and request your COE instantly.
  • By mail: Send VA Form 1880 to the VA.
  • Through your lender: Many lenders can request your COE directly via the VA system.

VA Loan Limits (Will This Cap Affect You?)

VA loans have limits on the amount the VA will guarantee:

  • In 2026, the VA loan limit is $801,500 for most of the U.S. (higher in expensive markets like California and New York).
  • You can borrow more than the VA limit, but the excess isn't guaranteed by the VA (the lender takes on more risk).
  • Most borrowers stay within the limit since rates are better and terms are easier within the guarantee.

VA vs Conventional vs FHA: Quick Comparison

  • VA: 0% down, no PMI, 0.25–0.5% lower rate, 50–60% DTI allowed.
  • Conventional: 5–20% down, PMI if under 20%, market rates, 43% DTI.
  • FHA: 3.5% down, FHA mortgage insurance (permanent if put down less than 10%), rates higher than VA, 50% DTI allowed.

Common VA Loan Misconceptions

  • Myth: "I can only use my VA loan once." Truth: You can reuse it multiple times, even on multiple properties (with some limits).
  • Myth: "I need to use my full benefit immediately." Truth: You can use it whenever you're ready—there's no expiration date.
  • Myth: "VA loans have higher interest rates." Truth: VA rates are typically lower than conventional rates.
  • Myth: "I can't refinance a VA loan into another VA loan." Truth: VA into VA refis are common and often called VA streamline refis.

The Bottom Line

If you're eligible for a VA loan, it's typically your best option. No down payment, no PMI, lower rates, and flexible lending criteria make VA loans one of the strongest benefits available to military families. Don't overlook this or underestimate its value—the financial advantage compounds over 30 years.
